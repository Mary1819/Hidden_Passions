Careers

Singer

    Education

  • Perform vocal arrangements of songs in front of live audiences
  • Collaborate with other singers in ensembles, bands or choirs
  • Practice their techniques through vocal lessons and rehearsals
  • Communicate/promote with their management group and audiences

    • Responsiblity

  • Musicians and singers perform music for live audiences and recordings. Audition for positions for music groups. Practice playing instruments or singing to improve their technique.

    • Salary

  • As of Apr 22, 2023, the average annual pay for a Professional Singer in the United States is $72,185 a year. Approximately $34.70 an hour. This is the equivalent of $1,388/week or $6,015/month.

Music Producer

    Education

  • Train your ears. Learn an instrument. Get a digital audio workstation. Have a production and recording space. Learn how to use an audio mixer. Network with artists. Consider a degree in music. Take an internship. Create music

    • Responsiblity

  • Music producers are professionals responsible for shaping the sound of individual music tracks and guiding the overall vision of an album.
  • Working with artists, hire studio musicians or work alone.
  • There is also a business component to the career and music producers often own and maintain their own studios, sell and promote music and offer mix and mastering services. Music producers can create beats alone, write entire songs, mix and master existing music, and arrange and sample music.

    • Salary

  • The music producer salary ranges between $49,080 and $61,519, with the average salary being $54,865.
