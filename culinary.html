Culinary

Culinary history isn't just a great way to gain access to tons of riveting stories and mouth watering tidbits about your favorite edibles. It's also a way to gain a unique perspective on human history itself. American culinary history books tell the story of how our society became everything that it is today. Teaches you how to motivate people to eat healthy food, how to make it healthy, and what components to add to make it so. It is also important to learn the kind of spices to use with specific foods.

Chef

  • Graduate with a high school diploma or culinary school certificate
  • Complete an apprenticeship program.
  • Work in the food service industry.

    • Responsibility

  • Create the weekly staff schedule
  • Adhering to the kitchen budget created by the manager
  • Holding staff meetings
  • Train all kitchen staff members
  • Maintaining relationships with vendors

Baker

    Education

  • Most bakers learn their skills through on-the-job training. The length of training varies but may last up to 1 year.
  • Training or apprenticeship programs cover topics such as baking and decorating techniques, production processes, and food safety.

    • Responsibility

  • Bakers mix ingredients according to recipes in order to make breads, pastries, and other baked goods.

    • Salary

  • As of Apr 22, 2023, the average hourly pay for a Baker in the United States is $14.90 an hour.
  • Hourly wages as high as $21.39 and as low as $8.17, the majority of Baker wages currently range between $12.02 to $16.83 across the United States.
